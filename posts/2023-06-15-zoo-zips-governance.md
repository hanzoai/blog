# ZIPs: Community-Driven Governance for Decentralized AI

Who decides what AI gets built? Today, a handful of corporate labs make that decision for all of humanity. The Zoo Labs Foundation was created to offer an alternative: community-governed AI research.

Today we are launching ZIPs — Zoo Improvement Proposals — the governance system for decentralized AI development.

## How ZIPs Work

ZIPs follow a structured process modeled on Bitcoin's BIP system and Ethereum's EIP system, adapted for AI research:

### Proposal Types

- **ZIP-Research**: New research directions, training runs, model architectures
- **ZIP-Protocol**: Changes to Zoo network protocols (DSO, PoAI, zLLM)
- **ZIP-Governance**: Changes to the governance process itself
- **ZIP-Resource**: Resource allocation decisions (compute budgets, grants)

### Process

1. **Draft**: Author submits a ZIP with motivation, specification, and resource requirements
2. **Review**: Community review period (minimum 14 days) with public discussion
3. **Vote**: Token-weighted voting with quorum requirements
4. **Implementation**: Approved ZIPs are funded and assigned to contributors
5. **Completion**: Results are published openly — successes and failures alike

### Published ZIPs

Key ZIPs already ratified:

- **ZIP-001 (DSO)**: Decentralized Semantic Optimization — zero-compute model adaptation through experience injection
- **ZIP-002 (PoAI)**: Proof of AI — verifiable AI compute as a consensus primitive
- **ZIP-003 (zLLM)**: Zoo LLM architecture — decentralized model serving and inference

## Why Governance Matters for AI

AI development without governance produces AI that serves the developers, not the users. Corporate labs optimize for engagement, revenue, and competitive advantage. The models they build reflect those priorities.

Zoo's governance ensures that:

- **Research priorities** are set by the community, not investors
- **Safety constraints** are decided transparently, not in closed-door meetings
- **Resources** are allocated based on merit, not politics
- **Results** are published openly, not hidden behind paywalls

## Participate

ZIPs are live at [zips.zoo.ngo](https://zips.zoo.ngo). Anyone can submit a proposal. Voting requires ZOO governance tokens. Discussion is open to all.
